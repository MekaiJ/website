CodeQuest 2025

  • Date: February 15, 2025
  • Location: EWU Catalyst Building Spokane, WA

This hackathon was run by the Google Developer Group at Eastern Washing University. The entire even lasted from 8:00am to 11:30pm. A notable part of the event was the tech talk from Gurbinder Gill, the co-founder and CPO of Corvic AI. This hackathon's theme was centered around the usage of AI to aid in creating a project.

Reflection

This was my first ever hackathon and I had an insane amount of fun. I teamed up with two other students at EWU, Luke Barnes and Liam Earl. We ended up creating a game with working LAN multiplayer, entirely in Java. As my group members and I jotted ideas down on the whiteboard, we realized we needed to prioritize features due to our time limit.

We ran into a lot of problems along the way, the biggest one being version control. All of us had used GitHub before for personal solo projects, but none of use had used it collaboratively. More than once, we had pushed something to main and after another person had synced their project, merge conflict after merge conflict showed up. I found this to be an incredible learning experience though, because these problems motivated me to research how to use git more effectively in a team environment.

In the end, I'm happy to say our team won first place! This hackathon was a blast and I look forward to more opportunities to compete in the future. This experience boosted my knowledge as a programmer and has made me love programming even more than I did before.
